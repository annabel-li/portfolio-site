About Me

Welcome to my engineering portfolio! My name is Annabel Li and I'm an undergraduate Engineering Physics student at the University of British Columbia.

I'm passionate about interdisciplinary projects - especially those involving software with physical applications - and love tackling new challenges. I am currently an ATLAS Deep-Learning Research Assistant at TRIUMF, Canada's particle physics center, where I'm studying the optimization and implementation of neural networks on hardware for the the Large Hadron Collider in Switzerland.

In addition to my work in machine learning, I also have hands-on mechanical experience as an aerodynamics member on UBC Formula SAE. Our team designs and builds a Formula One-style racecar from the ground up to compete at the the international SAE event in Michigan each May, a rigorous design cycle that involves testing, simulation, manufacturing, and validation. Last season, I designed and manufactured the beam wing for the 2025 vehicle, increasing rear wing downforce by ~10%.

If you have any questions about my work, or just want to reach out, feel free to connect - I'd love to hear from you!

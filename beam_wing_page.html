Beam Wing (2024-2025)

A lightweight aerodynamic component designed to enhance cornering performance and driver handling, designed for the 2024-2025 UBC Formula SAE vehicle.

Beam wing (yellow element) shown above on the final 2025 vehicle.

Affiliated with: University of British Columbia - Formula SAE design team

Project timeline: Sept. 2024 - May 2025

Key technical skills: SolidWorks, Star CCM+, CNC machining, carbon fiber



Context

Racecar aerodynamics has two main goals: maximize downforce (which increases grip), while minimizing drag. A beam wing is a small, isolated element located on the rear wing of the car, which can boost downforce with minimal contributions to drag.


Goals

Improve the vehicle's aerodynamic balance by appreciably increasing rear downforce

Design a component that minimially impacts manufacturing timelines and cost


Process

Sept. - Dec 2025:

  • Examined last year's configuration and supplemented with external research to identify potential designs
  • Configured designs with CAD (SolidWorks), using Star CCM+ to test and analyze results
  • Presented findings in weekly meetings and in a team-wide design review in November, incorporating feedback and updating workflows in context of other members' reports


Jan. - May 2025:

  • Cut, glued, and CNCed airfoil molds out of MDF
  • Used molds to perform carbon fiber layups, adding internal structures after curing

Impacts & Reflection

  • Increased rear wing downforce by ~10%
  • Learned how to optimize standalone designs holistically, considering system-wide integration and time and manufacturing costs
